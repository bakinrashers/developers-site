Join the Guardian’s Development Team

We’re committed to a digital-first strategy, and in order to achieve this, we’re expanding our team of developers.

We need people who love a challenge and are willing to successfully take on difficult tasks, contribute to their team and learn on the job. Our engineers enjoy learning and sharing what they learn and they get to work on some unique challenges.

We work in cross-functional agile teams that we trust to tackle the problems and objectives that they are given. All development is based in London in the same offices as our journalists and editors.

Software Engineer

We are seeking excellent and passionate engineers to work on projects across our business. Our main news website theguardian.com, which serves millions of readers each day, has traffic peaks of up to 1200 pages per second and close to 100% uptime. Our Open Platform makes our content available not just to our internal websites and mobile applications but also many other partners and developers.

We have many projects in progress simultaneously and our teams are given the autonomy to choose the right technologies to solve their problems. Our core platforms are Scala, JavaScript, Play and Amazon Web Services.

Our developers are continually developing their skills and are eager to learn new things, displaying the aptitude to change and respond to a changing news situation. They are at the forefront of our work to create a Guardian for the new century.

Client-side Developer

You’ll be an expert with CSS3 and modern JavaScript, comfortable writing OOCSS and using technologies such as AMD and CommonJS, and thinking and working in a responsive, progressively-enhanced manner. We’re looking for creative Client-side Developers with good knowledge of scalability and performance. Knowledge of HTML5 and CSS3 is a must, and an eye for design and/or UX would be a bonus. Knowledge and experience of automated or multi-platform testing is desirable, too.

Interactive Developer

The Guardian is seeking a talented Interactive Developer with a passion for interactive storytelling and data visualisation to join its award-winning newsroom interactive graphics team.

In this role, you will be expected to conceive of, design and develop ambitious pieces of visual journalism. You will work closely with reporters and editors on a range of editorial projects, from breaking news to investigations.

Your work will expand the reach and resonance of our reporting, helping readers better understand complex issues and furthering the Guardian's goal to be the world’s most trusted source for high-quality, independent journalism.

To succeed, you must have a track record of producing innovative pieces of interactive storytelling. You also must have expert-level front-end coding skills: HTML, CSS and JavaScript. Also critical is attention to detail, a pride in the quality of one's work and the ability to meet news deadlines. Experience with data-visualisation libraries like D3 or Raphaël is strongly preferred.

Although journalism experience is not a requirement, you must have a good understanding of the Guardian, an interest in current affairs and a strong desire to work in news.
